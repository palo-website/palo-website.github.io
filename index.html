Abstract

Learned language-conditioned robot policies fail to effectively adapt to new real-world tasks even when pre-trained across a diverse set of instructions. We propose a novel approach for few-shot adaptation to unseen tasks that exploits the semantic understanding of task decomposition provided by new capabilities of vision-language models (VLMs). Our method, Policy Adaptation via Language Optimization (PALO), combines a handful of demonstrations of a task with proposed language decompositions sampled from a VLM to quickly enable rapid nonparametric adaptation, avoiding the need for a larger fine-tuning dataset. We evaluate PALO on extensive real-world experiments consisting of challenging unseen, long-horizon robot manipulation tasks. We find that PALO is able of consistently complete long-horizon, multi-tier tasks in the real world, outperforming state of the art pre-trained generalist policies, and methods that have access to the same demonstrations

Policy Adaptation via Language Optimization

Given only a few (n=5) expert demonstrations, PALO evaluates the best language decomposition proposals generated from VLMs using nonparametric optimization and take said optimal language decomposition plan with high and low level instructions during inference.

Training a Hierarchical Language-Conditioned Agent

To utilize the hierarchical language decomposition, we train a language-conditioned behavioral cloning agent with algorithmically generated low level instruction within the training dataset. We also mask out low and high level instructions during training.

Unlike prior configurations, this architecture allows our policy to be jointly conditioned on high and low level language instructions, allowing the policy to finish tasks with either modality.

Performance Comparison

While evaluating on long-horizon and unseen skills tasks, PALO outperforms all conventional zero-shot generalization methods by 3x in terms of success rate, and carries positive improvement compared to conventional finetuning methods despite having only 5 expert demonstrations per task.

Unlike previous finetuning methods that take up to 30 minutes to train given the dataset size, PALO is also computationally efficient, taking up only 470 seconds for optimization to finish.

Key Components

  • Using a pretrained VLM helps tremendously with task decomposition in achieving OOD tasks.
  • Conditioning on different language modalities (high vs low) give different benefits on different scenes.
  • Stochastic boundary sampling gives better language decomposition.
